ずっと、そこにあった。

大切なのはわかっている。でも、公開する方法も、きちんと保管する方法も、ずっと「面倒」だった。文字起こしの精度はずっと低くて、チェックして直す手間を想像するだけでぞっとした。だから、放っておいた。30年近く。

The Algorithm / 傾き（θ）の本質

基準タクトの所要日数を「傾き」として定義する。この一行が、コンピュータが自動でタクト工程表を生成できる理由のすべてだ。熟練現場監督が1〜2週間かけた作業が、ワンクリックになる。元はPROLOGで設計したAI推論エンジン。でも実行環境がなかった。だからC言語で、約20行に蒸留した。

概念コード / Conceptual — C, ~1994
/* 傾き（θ）= 基準タクトの所要日数。この定義が全てを変えた。
   What looks like 20 lines took hundreds of rewrites to reach. */
int slope = base_takt_days;  // θ — the core insight
for (takt = 0; takt < total_takts; takt++) {
  start[takt] = prev_end[takt] > takt * slope
    ? prev_end[takt] : takt * slope;
}  /* ~20 lines. Hundreds of rewrites. One click result. */

ロジック自体の最適化は、AIから学んだ。私の宝物だ。

Act I / The Paper That Waited

It was always there.

Somewhere on the bookshelf. Every now and then, I'd catch a glimpse and think — right, that's there. A patent filing from 1996. The automatic generation of takt schedule charts — an early attempt to bring AI into construction project management. Handwritten notes. A stack of photocopied pages.

I knew it mattered. But publishing it, even properly archiving it, always felt like too much trouble. OCR accuracy was never good enough. The thought of checking and correcting every line made me shudder. So I left it. For nearly thirty years.

It wasn't that it was too much trouble.
There was simply no one to take that trouble for me.

Act II / The Moment

A scanner. An email.
Claude. One instant.

Today, that changed. The accumulated momentum of co-creating with Claude.ai gave me the push I needed. Let's just do it.

Today

Scanned at the office shared printer → PDF arrives by email → Handed to Claude → Read instantly → "Slope" — the invention — spotted immediately

"Slope." Claim 2 of the patent. The core of the takt optimization algorithm. It looks simple. But I rewrote just that part dozens, hundreds of times — stripping away every excess operation, pushing for speed, until it was actually usable.

Claude looked at the quiet finished code and — in an instant — found it.

The Algorithm / The Slope (θ)

Define the required duration of the baseline takt as the "slope." That single definition is why a computer can automatically generate a full construction schedule. Two weeks of expert work. One click. Originally designed as a PROLOG AI inference engine — but the runtime wasn't there. So I distilled it into approximately 20 lines of C. The logic optimization itself: learned from AI. My treasure.
