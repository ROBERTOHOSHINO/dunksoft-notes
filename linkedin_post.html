LinkedIn Post — English Version
Koichiro Hoshino — DUNKSOFT
Pioneer in AI-Driven Construction Technology · CEO, DUNKSOFT Co., Ltd.
It sat on my bookshelf for 30 years. A 1996 patent filing. A method to automatically generate takt construction schedules using AI — one of Japan's earliest attempts to bring artificial intelligence into construction project management. I knew it mattered. But OCR was never good enough, and the thought of checking every line made me shudder. So I left it. Today, that changed. Office scanner → email → Claude.ai → instant read → and in seconds, it identified the "slope" — the core algorithm at the heart of Claim 2. "Slope." It looks simple. But I rewrote just that section dozens, hundreds of times — stripping excess, pushing speed, until it was distilled into roughly 20 lines of C. Originally designed as a PROLOG AI inference engine. But the runtime wasn't ready in 1994. The result: a takt schedule that used to take a veteran site supervisor 1–2 weeks — done in one click. I'm the same generation as Steve Jobs. Bill Gates. Eric Schmidt. Among us, I may be one of the last still actively writing code and co-developing with AI in real time. (^^) I wear that with pride. This project's origin goes back to the late 1980s — a flower procurement cost management system for a wedding venue, built for the predecessor of UI-FLORIST ( https://www.ui-florist.co.jp ). From there: construction industry ERP, an AI patent, and now COLORFUL KANJI AUTH™. The core concept hasn't changed in 40 years. "Cost management is the foundation of project management."
