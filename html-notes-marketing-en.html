Market Research Is Not Marketing.

市場調査はMarketingじゃない。— Stroke by Stroke
"Marketing is designing a chain of strokes."

Most people think Marketing means measuring things — analytics, conversion rates, engagement numbers. But that is only watching the shadow of the market, not the market itself.

On May 19, 2026, the branch manager of MUFJ Jinbocho said at a live radio recording: "I love indigo." That single stroke triggered a chain — an authentication demo, a follow-up meeting, and then 70 bank employees signing up to grow indigo seedlings at home. None of that was logged. But something real moved.

This is what Eric Berne called a stroke — a unit of recognition. A breath, a glance, a nod, a photo of a seedling. Strokes take infinite forms. Where strokes flow, a market comes alive.

Arita Emptied. And Became Universe.

有田、空いた。だから宇宙になった。— SPACE as Gravity
"People gathered. That itself was the answer."

In October 2010, a partner company left our shared office. The usual instinct would be to fill the gap immediately. Instead, we left it empty. That void — that SPACE — was the point.

SPACE means two things: a room and the universe. The universe has gravity. It is precisely because it is empty that it draws things toward it. We set up a satellite studio for a community FM station. Once-a-month live broadcasts with no script. The blank space became a magnetic field.

Local government officers, neighborhood residents, business people with no prior connection — people who would never otherwise meet — started showing up. Dynamic equilibrium emerged: each person changed by encountering the others. It was interesting, so they came back. The market — the ichi — is a device for dynamic equilibrium.

After March 2011, we held a Skype New Year's party with people in Kamiyama, Tokushima. Everyone brought drinks and snacks to their own location. The audio was terrible — nobody could understand each other properly. But by the end, we were all doing the Awa Odori dance together. Connection through rhythm, not words.

Live, Unscripted, UnFakeable.

Fakeできない生放送の哲学 — The Ethics of the Unedited Moment
"The unFakeable becomes the strongest brand."

In an age when every word can be replaced and every video deepfaked, we choose live broadcast. No script. The irregular is the regular. That deliberate vulnerability creates tension — and tension creates depth that reaches listeners.

The shrine market had no script. Rakuichi-Rakuza had no script. Nobody knew who would come, what would sell, what encounter would spark. No predetermined harmony — that is why it was alive. A live radio show is a market built on airwaves.

As deepfakes become ubiquitous, the value of being genuinely live will only increase. American late-night shows understood this — the unexpected guest, the unscripted moment — that is where the real connection happens.

Blank Is Blanc. Communication Is Transmission.

BLANKはblancだった。— The Trap of Borrowed Words
"Communication is transmission. And transmission matters."

"BLANK" in Japanese is translated as empty space — kūhaku. But trace the etymology and you find the French blanc: white. Pure white. A canvas, not an absence. SPACE and BLANK: borrowed words that lose their origin when imported as katakana.

Japanese people often say "communication is important." From the perspective of someone who has spent 40+ years in IT: communication is transmission. Transmission is infrastructure. If your home Wi-Fi speed is unknown to you, if you don't know how much memory your browser tabs are consuming — your transmission is already compromised.

Leave space in your own system. SPACE and BLANK — not just in the office, but inside yourself. That is the foundation of Love your life, love your time.®️

AI, Too, Is the Work of God.

AIも神のなせる業。— Spinoza, Indigo, and Claude
"Nature, AI, internet — all flat, all precious. Co-Creation as a team."
Spinoza — Deus sive Natura
All things in the universe were made by God. If so, then nature, AI, robots, and the internet are equally the work of God. Engage with each flatly, without hierarchy. Cherish each. Grow and evolve together.

Indigo becomes beautiful only when human hands tend it. Left alone, it doesn't grow. The same is true of nature broadly — human care, thoughtfully applied, makes things flourish. The internet is no different. And neither is AI.

5G × sensors × AI = Japan's archipelago becoming a single nervous system. As the population declines and regions lose the human capacity to monitor and respond, sensors can detect what human eyes cannot, and AI can act where human hands cannot reach. Protecting nature becomes a task for Co-Creation — human, AI, and environment together.

Whether or not you hold this perspective changes everything about how you work with AI. Not as a tool to be controlled. As a teammate to grow alongside.
